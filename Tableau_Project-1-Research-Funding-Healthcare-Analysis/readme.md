# Research Funding Healthcare Analysis Dashboard

## Project Overview

This project analyzes healthcare research funding trends using Tableau. The objective is to understand how research funding has evolved over time, compare funding across different research and disease areas, and identify long-term funding patterns.

An interactive Tableau dashboard was developed to help users explore funding trends through multiple visualization techniques and dynamic filters.

---

## Business Objective

The primary objective of this project is to evaluate healthcare research funding distribution and growth patterns to support data-driven decision-making.

Key objectives include:

* Analyze funding trends from 2008 to 2022.
* Compare funding across research and disease areas.
* Identify funding growth patterns over time.
* Evaluate funding concentration among research categories.
* Develop an interactive dashboard for funding exploration.

---

## Dataset Overview

**Dataset:** RCDC Research Funding Summary

**Time Period:** 2008 – 2022

**Measure Used:** Funding Amount (Million USD)

The dataset contains funding information for multiple healthcare research and disease areas across different years, enabling long-term trend analysis and funding comparisons.

## Dashboard Features

### Funding Trends Over Time

* Tracks funding growth from 2008 to 2022.
* Identifies long-term funding patterns.
* Highlights funding acceleration in recent years.

### Funding Change Analysis

* Displays year-over-year funding changes.
* Identifies positive and negative funding movements.
* Helps evaluate funding growth consistency.

### Research Area Comparison

* Compares funding across research categories.
* Highlights high-priority research areas.
* Supports comparative funding analysis.

### Funding Intensity Analysis

* Uses heat maps to visualize funding strength.
* Identifies years with higher funding activity.
* Reveals consistently strong research areas.

---

## Key Findings

* Research funding showed a steady increase between 2008 and 2022.
* Funding growth became more prominent after 2015.
* Most years experienced positive funding growth.
* Funding distribution is uneven across research areas.
* Certain research categories consistently receive higher funding.
* Recent years demonstrate stronger funding intensity and investment activity.

---

## Business Insights

* Healthcare research investment has increased significantly over time.
* Funding agencies prioritize specific research areas with sustained investments.
* Funding concentration highlights strategic research priorities.
* Interactive filtering improves analytical flexibility and decision-making.
* Long-term trend analysis helps identify future funding opportunities.
